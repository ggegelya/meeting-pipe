## Progress (2026-07-17, partial)

**Leg (b) shipped.** `SegmentBuilder.coalesce` (applied by `FluidAudioRunner` right after `SegmentBuilder.build`, so the split primitive keeps its own tests) drops phantom punctuation-only rows (Unicode-aware, so Cyrillic stays speech) and merges consecutive same-speaker fragments into readable turns, bounded by a 1.2 s inter-segment gap and a 30 s max-turn cap. 11 new `SegmentBuilderTests` cases. This is the raw `<stem>.json` seam every downstream reader (Library rows, markers, per-segment reassignment, `--der`/`--attribution`) shares; the Python `render_markdown` already merged same-speaker blocks for the `.md` only.

**Leg (a) partial.** Two hypotheses were resolved by reading FluidAudio 0.14.8:
- The `stepRatio` (0.1 vs 0.2) the spec names lives in `OfflineDiarizerConfig` / `OfflineDiarizerManager`. The daemon runs the *online* `DiarizerManager` (`performCompleteDiarization`), whose only clustering dial is `clusteringThreshold` (0.7 default; lower = more speakers). Getting the stepRatio lever means migrating to the offline diarizer (different API + result type that FEAT3 voiceprints depend on): spike-sized, not done.
- Removing `speakerManager.reset()` is the wrong fix: the 5-person merge in `20260716-103024` was *within one meeting*, and `reset()` only clears the *cross-meeting* database at job start (the deliberate ASR2 anti-drift fix). Removing it reintroduces that drift and does not touch within-meeting merges. Rejected.

What shipped for leg (a): a wired `transcription.diarization_clustering_threshold` config knob (daemon `Config` -> `TranscriptionService.makeRunner` -> `FluidAudioRunner` -> `DiarizerConfig`), clamped to FluidAudio's 0.5-0.9 range, default lowered from 0.7 to **0.65** in the safer failure direction (an over-split shows an unnamed `THEM-A`/`THEM-B` the owner merges in-app; a merge asserts a confidently wrong attendee / action owner, the broken promise). The knob makes the threshold sweepable against the corpus without a rebuild.

**Owner-owed (why this stays `partial`):** the right threshold value cannot be picked here (2 test meetings on this Mac; the real signal is the owner's hand-labelled corpus). Sweep `diarization_clustering_threshold` down (0.6, 0.55...) across a few real meetings and read `scripts/valid1_check.py --der`'s "MERGED CLUSTERS" line, which rides on the corrections the owner makes anyway. Promote to done once a value holds the merge closed without over-splitting; re-band or open a `/spike` if the offline-diarizer migration (for stepRatio + community-1's lower DER) is worth pursuing.

**DIAR2 (P1, new): diarization + segmentation quality.** Raised by the owner while hand-labelling speakers, then measured. Two legs, one severe.

**(a) The diarizer merges several people into one voice.** In `20260716-103024`, cluster `THEM-A` (which the owner named "Sudip") in fact held **five people**: Sudip, Anisha, Heorhii, Yash, Ayush. The owner re-separated 20 lines by hand, which is why `scripts/valid1_check.py --der` scores that meeting **49% corrected** against under 1% for spot-checked meetings. Worse, the owner's own voice was inside the merge: `diarize.label_me_speaker` pulled out only 3 segments and left 6 more buried in `THEM-A`, so the self-voiceprint did not rescue it either. This is not cosmetic: the summary's attendees and its action-item / decision owners are read off these labels, so a merge like this makes the summary confidently name the wrong person. That is why this is filed P1 (a broken core promise) rather than as polish, though it is quality work with an uncertain fix, so the owner may re-band it. `--der`'s "MERGED CLUSTERS" line is the regression detector; it needs no extra labelling because it rides on corrections the owner makes anyway. Worth checking first: whether the clustering threshold is simply too loose, whether `speakerManager.reset()` per job (ASR2) throws away the cross-meeting evidence that would separate them, and whether the FluidAudio step ratio (0.1 vs 0.2, see VALID1: 0.1 is effectively free at a median 91x real time) moves it.

**(b) Segmentation noise and over-splitting.** Across the 2026-07-15/16 meetings, **7.9% of segments are punctuation only** (`.`, at 0.16-0.32 s), and **10.3% are 3 characters or shorter**; median segment is 2.64 s but p10 is 0.32 s. The `.` rows are almost certainly noise the ASR resolves into phantom punctuation, and they clutter the transcript, waste summary context, and give the diarizer near-empty spans to attribute. Sentences also split mid-thought (owner screenshot). Scope: drop or merge sub-threshold / punctuation-only segments at the `SegmentBuilder` seam, and consider merging adjacent same-speaker fragments into readable turns. Cheap relative to (a), and independently verifiable from the corpus with the counts above as the baseline.
