# DIAR2: Diarization + segmentation quality

Band origin: filed 2026-07-16 (owner-raised, then measured). Status and priority live in this task's ToC row in [meetingpipe-q6-backlog.md](../meetingpipe-q6-backlog.md).

**DIAR2 (P1, new): diarization + segmentation quality.** Raised by the owner while hand-labelling speakers, then measured. Two legs, one severe.

**(a) The diarizer merges several people into one voice.** In `20260716-103024`, cluster `THEM-A` (which the owner named "Sudip") in fact held **five people**: Sudip, Anisha, Heorhii, Yash, Ayush. The owner re-separated 20 lines by hand, which is why `scripts/valid1_check.py --der` scores that meeting **49% corrected** against under 1% for spot-checked meetings. Worse, the owner's own voice was inside the merge: `diarize.label_me_speaker` pulled out only 3 segments and left 6 more buried in `THEM-A`, so the self-voiceprint did not rescue it either. This is not cosmetic: the summary's attendees and its action-item / decision owners are read off these labels, so a merge like this makes the summary confidently name the wrong person. That is why this is filed P1 (a broken core promise) rather than as polish, though it is quality work with an uncertain fix, so the owner may re-band it. `--der`'s "MERGED CLUSTERS" line is the regression detector; it needs no extra labelling because it rides on corrections the owner makes anyway. Worth checking first: whether the clustering threshold is simply too loose, whether `speakerManager.reset()` per job (ASR2) throws away the cross-meeting evidence that would separate them, and whether the FluidAudio step ratio (0.1 vs 0.2, see VALID1: 0.1 is effectively free at a median 91x real time) moves it.

**(b) Segmentation noise and over-splitting.** Across the 2026-07-15/16 meetings, **7.9% of segments are punctuation only** (`.`, at 0.16-0.32 s), and **10.3% are 3 characters or shorter**; median segment is 2.64 s but p10 is 0.32 s. The `.` rows are almost certainly noise the ASR resolves into phantom punctuation, and they clutter the transcript, waste summary context, and give the diarizer near-empty spans to attribute. Sentences also split mid-thought (owner screenshot). Scope: drop or merge sub-threshold / punctuation-only segments at the `SegmentBuilder` seam, and consider merging adjacent same-speaker fragments into readable turns. Cheap relative to (a), and independently verifiable from the corpus with the counts above as the baseline.
