**[DONE] PERF5 (P3): 4 Hz engine-tick gating.** Shipped 2026-07-11. Original scope in the ToC row above. `MeetingLifecycleCoordinator` armed a `tickInterval` (0.25 s = 4 Hz) `Timer` in `engage` and ran it for the whole meeting, even though `PromotionEngine.tick` only advances an `.endingProvisional` toward `.ended` on the debounce and is a no-op in every other phase, so it woke `engineQueue` four times a second for the entire `.starting` / `.inMeeting` span to do nothing. Fix: `PromotionEngine` exposes `hasPendingEndDeadline` (true only in `.endingProvisional`), and a new `reconcileTick(generation:)` arms or disarms the timer to match. It runs on `engineQueue` after every engine mutation (`ingest`, `tick`, `confirmRecording`, `confirmProvisionalEnd`), samples the pending state on that queue (the only one that mutates the engine), then hops to main to touch the `Timer` and the main-only `cancelTick`, guarded by the AUD-29 session generation so a superseded session's late reconcile is dropped; `engage` no longer arms unconditionally and `disengage` still cancels directly. The arm/disarm is idempotent, so overlapping mutations converge. Net: the tick fires only while a provisional end is pending (seconds per meeting, not its whole length). The poll-backoff remainder for the three 1 Hz pollers had already shipped; this closes the 4 Hz leg. Verified: daemon 1114 green, incl. two new coordinator tests asserting the tick arms on a provisional end and disarms on both the promote-to-`.ended` and the flicker-revert-to-`.inMeeting` paths; no behaviour change (the manual `coordinator.tick()` drive and every existing lifecycle assertion are unchanged).
