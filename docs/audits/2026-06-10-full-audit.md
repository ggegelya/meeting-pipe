# Full application audit, 2026-06-10

**Scope:** implementation, security, UI/UX, tests/CI, architecture, plus a competitor scan. Recurring problem areas (start detection, end detection, mute gate) audited for what the existing TECH-MIC / TECH-END bands do NOT already track.

**Method:** a 53-agent workflow: 1 backlog ground-truth agent built a known-issues exclusion list; 10 domain finders produced evidence-cited findings; every critical/major finding went to an independent adversarial refuter (read the cited code, attempt to refute, re-judge severity and confidence); 2 web agents researched competitors (Granola, Notion AI, Fathom, Otter, Krisp, MacWhisper, Superwhisper, screenpipe, Hyprnote). 40 findings were verified; 10 were refuted, downgraded to minor, or marked duplicates of known backlog items. Confidence = the refuter's posterior (0-100) that the finding is real and matters as stated; anything below 85 carries the reason.

**Repo-state caveat:** the entire TECH-END band (END1-END4 + review fixes, commits 742efef..35fa406) landed on `main` from a parallel session WHILE this audit ran. Every end-detection finding below was re-verified by hand against HEAD `35fa406`; findings the END band already fixed are marked superseded, and one new finding (AUD-9) is a defect the END3 replacement inherited.

**Ground truth runs (2026-06-10):** pipeline `ruff` clean, `pytest` 301/301 in 7.4s. Daemon CI red since 2026-06-06 (see AUD-21). Local `main` is 10 commits ahead of `origin/main`, all unevaluated by CI.

## Executive summary

The five load-bearing problems, in pick-up order:

1. **The prompt/suppression lifecycle can wedge detection off permanently** (AUD-1/2): a skipped or timed-out prompt plus one stale AX event leaves the daemon in `.suppressed` until restart, silently missing every later meeting. This is the start-side complement of the END band and is untouched by it.
2. **Captured audio can be destroyed after a successful meeting** (AUD-5): the ffmpeg merge result is never checked and the only intermediates are deleted unconditionally. One broken Homebrew ffmpeg upgrade = every meeting lost at stop time, after a perfect capture.
3. **Crash-orphaned meetings strip their privacy routing** (AUD-6): the meta sidecar exists only at clean stop, so a BYO or NDA meeting interrupted by a crash/rebuild is auto-summarized via Anthropic and published to Notion at next launch. Privacy promise broken by a code path, not a policy.
4. **Failures are systematically silent** (AUD-7/13/14/15/16): disk-full and 4 GiB-cap write failures, mid-meeting SCStream death, all-sinks-failed publishes, stranded queue jobs, and timeout-skips all produce zero user-visible signal at the moment of loss. Competitor evidence says silent capture degradation, not detection, is the top real-world failure of this product class.
5. **CI is red and parts of the safety net silently skip** (AUD-21/22): snapshot references were born OS-mismatched with the runner (never green), and the MuteRedactor data-safety tests skip on CI because the runner lacks ffmpeg. The most destructive code path in the app is gate-checked by tests that do not run.

Browser meetings deserve a special call-out (AUD-3): plain-tab Meet/Teams/Zoom meetings are structurally undiscoverable today (URL fragments are matched against window titles, which carry page titles, never URLs). For a product whose ambition is "works across Zoom/Teams/Slack/Meet", the default join-from-calendar-link flow in a browser cannot trigger detection at all.

What is genuinely solid (verified-holding): the MIC4/MIC5 capture-first plumbing (fail-closed CaptureMode, timeline frame-clock across device pads, redactor idempotency + originals fail-safe), TECH-SEC3's egress chokepoint (httpx transport covers anthropic + notion + local), SEC2 sink clamp, SEC5 env strip, the empty-transcript guards, and FEAT5's auto fallback including double-failure surfacing.

## Critical findings

### AUD-1: Skip/timeout + one stale AX end event wedges the daemon in `.suppressed`; all future meetings silently missed
**Severity: critical | Confidence: 88 | Area: detection/architecture | Survives at HEAD 35fa406**
While the prompt is up or after Skip, the PromotionEngine sits in `.starting`. In `.starting`, the FIRST `.ended` event from any signal goes terminal with `confirmedBy` hardcoded empty (`PromotionEngine.handleStarting`, the END2 corroboration guard covers only the `tick()` path). If that event is an AX leave-button invalidation (sub-100ms push, beats the polls; the Teams mini-window and the 2026-06-09 screen-share repro both emit exactly this), `PromptEndPolicy` holds the `.prompting/.suppressed` app state, but the engine is already terminally `.ended` and absorbs every later event. The genuine end can never produce a verdict, `.suppressed` never clears (discovery is gated on `.idle`), and the menu bar shows idle. Evidence: `PromotionEngine.swift:169-178` (`.starting` + first `.ended` goes terminal, no corroboration), `PromotionEngine.swift:115-116` (`.ended` absorbing), `PromptEndPolicy.swift:27-30`, `MeetingSessionController.swift:425-431`. Below 90 only because trigger frequency depends on the AX leg being armed during a prompt.
**Fix shape:** extend `requiresCorroboration` to `handleStarting`; re-engage or reset the engine when `PromptEndPolicy` withholds an end.

### AUD-2: A skipped Teams prompt suppresses ALL detection (every app) for as long as any Teams window exists
**Severity: critical | Confidence: 90 | Area: detection/architecture | Survives at HEAD**
Recovery from `.suppressed` requires a lifecycle `.ended`. For Teams in `.starting` the engaged signal set is: shareable-content matched by the universal "microsoft teams" token (any Teams window keeps it live, including chat), process-audio (retired by END1), and the AX leave button (rarely armed at discovery; if armed it produces AUD-1 instead). Net: skip one Teams meeting and prompt + auto-consent detection is suppressed globally, for every app, until Teams quits or the daemon restarts. Suppression is global, not per-bundle (`State.swift:57-62`, `MeetingSessionController.swift:378-379,578`). Evidence: `LifecycleAdapter.swift:94-99` (universal suffix token), `NativeLifecycleAdapter.swift:122` (same matcher is the SCK live gate), `PromotionEngine.swift:134-139` (`.starting` absorbs `.live` forever).
**Fix shape:** scope suppression per-bundle with a TTL or discovery-driven re-check; stop using the any-window token as the SCK liveness gate (require call-shaped titles for liveness, keep the loose token for discovery only).

### AUD-3: Plain browser-tab meetings are structurally undiscoverable
**Severity: critical | Confidence: 88 | Area: detection/implementation**
Browser candidates are admitted ONLY when a `meeting_apps.toml` URL fragment (`meet.google.com`, `zoom.us/j/`, ...) appears inside an AX window title; mainstream browsers put the page title there, never the URL. A Meet/Teams-web/Zoom-web meeting in a regular tab can never become a candidate: no prompt, no recording, every time. The PWA path is separate (and has the inverse problem, AUD-4). Grep confirms no `kAXURLAttribute`/`AXWebArea` read exists anywhere in the daemon. Evidence: `meeting_apps.toml:16-25` (URL values under a comment calling them "title fragments"), `MeetingSourceScanner.swift:93-94, 204-216`. Consistent with 0 browser meetings in 19.8 days of events.
**Fix shape:** match browser windows with the same page-title patterns the lifecycle adapter already has (`MeetingTitlePatterns.googleMeet/teams/...`), as the scanner already does for PWAs; optionally read the tab URL via the browser AX web area later. Pairs with (does not duplicate) END5/MIC7, which assume discovery works.

### AUD-4: An idle Meet/Teams PWA (open, not in a call) prompts to record; browsers are exempt from the corroboration gate
**Severity: major-on-the-edge-of-critical | Confidence: 93 | Area: detection**
A meeting-named PWA at its landing page is admitted by `localizedName` alone, stamped with a synthetic `titleMatch`, exempted from the lone-candidate corroboration gate (`.native`-only check), and its SCK liveness closure short-circuits on `isMeetingPWA`, so the false prompt persists while the PWA is open, and a timeout-skip then triggers AUD-2's suppression. Runtime events confirm it fired on this machine. Evidence: `MeetingSourceScanner.swift:115-130`, `BrowserMeetingLifecycleAdapter.swift:47-53, 114-126`, `MeetingSourceScorer.swift:78-85`.
**Fix shape:** keep name-matching for enumeration, require an in-call corroborator before prompting on name-only admissions, drop the `isMeetingPWA` bypass from the SCK closure.

### AUD-5: stop() and orphan recovery delete the only intermediates without verifying the ffmpeg merge produced a final WAV
**Severity: critical | Confidence: 90 | Area: data loss**
`runFFmpeg` discards the termination status; `stop()` then unconditionally deletes `.mic.wav` and `.system.wav`. Any merge failure (broken Homebrew ffmpeg after a dylib bump, disk full, kill mid-merge, malformed orphan input) destroys the only copies of a fully captured meeting. `recoverOrphan` deletes them BEFORE its own output-existence check, then logs "produced no file" after the data is gone. The no-ffmpeg fallback keeps mic-only and still deletes `system.wav` (everyone else's audio). The in-repo proof this is fixable is MuteRedactor, which does the same dance safely (exit-status check, restore on failure, idempotency). Evidence: `MeetingRecorder.swift:914-917, 1046-1051, 1065-1068, 1124-1128`.
**Fix shape:** make `runFFmpeg` return status; verify output exists with plausible size; delete intermediates only on verified success; on failure keep them, write a failure sidecar, notify.

### AUD-6: Crash-orphaned BYO/NDA meetings auto-egress to Anthropic + Notion at next launch
**Severity: critical | Confidence: 85 (88 and 82 from two independent domains) | Area: security/privacy**
The meta sidecar (workflow, NDA flag, BYO summary mode, source) is written only at clean stop; the shutdown flush is dead code (`applicationWillTerminate` calls `exit()` on the line after a fire-and-forget `Task { await recorder.stop() }`; no SIGTERM handler). At next launch, orphan recovery merges the audio, deletes the `.capturemode` marker, and enqueues with `summaryMode: .auto`; the pipeline sees no sidecar and applies global defaults (anthropic + notion). The capture-first quarantine covers only `.captureFirstRedact` orphans; `regulatedGate` orphans sail through. A daemon rebuild mid-meeting is a routine event for this user. Confidence below 90 only because the worst leg (NDA) requires an NDA workflow to exist at crash time; the BYO-to-auto rewrite is unconditional in code. Evidence: `App.swift:130-139`, `Coordinator.swift:423-427, 442-447`, `MeetingSessionController.swift:300,325`, `OrphanRecordingRecovery.swift:113-117`.
**Fix shape:** write the meta sidecar (or a start-time manifest: workflow id, NDA, summaryMode) at recording START; on recovery, quarantine or zero-egress-process any orphan whose manifest shows BYO/NDA/regulated; make termination stop synchronously.

### AUD-7: Per-buffer WAV write failures are swallowed; disk-full or the 4 GiB WAVE cap silently truncates while the HUD shows live levels
**Severity: critical | Confidence: 85 | Area: data loss**
Both writers catch write errors, log to os_log (not even `recording.log`), and drop the buffer; nothing counts failures, stops the recording, or tells the user. Disk-full mid-meeting records nothing for hours while the HUD meter (computed pre-write) dances. The Float32 system intermediate hits the RIFF u32 cap at ~3h06m (384 kB/s), after which every write fails the same silent way; an all-day workshop loses all system audio past hour three. The one post-stop parity check writes a WARN to a log file only. Below 90: the exact CoreAudio failure mode at the RIFF boundary is documented-but-not-reproduced here. Evidence: `MeetingRecorder.swift:277-283, 349-355, 1167-1169`, `SystemAudioCapture.swift:18-25` (rate math).
**Fix shape:** count consecutive write failures per writer; on threshold emit `recording_error`, degraded HUD + notification, force-stop to preserve the intact prefix. For the cap: segment-roll near 4 GiB or CAF intermediates.

### AUD-8: CI has been red since June 6 and was never green for snapshots: references are OS-pinned to the dev machine (macOS 26) vs the macos-15 runner
**Severity: critical-for-process | Confidence: 95 | Area: tests/CI**
The snapshot suite never passed on CI: references were recorded on macOS 26 (Tahoe), the runner is macos-15, and cross-OS-major SwiftUI rendering exceeds the configured precision. The batch push (four commits, one run on a docs-only head) manufactured the "a docs commit broke CI" illusion. Red-as-normal means the gate cannot signal a real regression: 10 local commits are currently unevaluated. Additionally the MuteRedactor data-safety tests (idempotency, the exact one-click-loss class MIC9 fixed) silently `XCTSkip` on CI because the runner has no ffmpeg, so the most destructive path has no enforced gate (verified: skip lines in the CI log). Evidence: `SnapshotTests.swift:12-13,31`, `ci.yml:159`, reference PNG mtimes == commit 254ab01, run history.
**Fix shape:** `XCTSkipIf(CI)` for snapshots (keep them a local tool) or OS-suffixed reference names; `brew install ffmpeg` in CI so redactor tests actually run; treat skips of safety tests as failures.

## Major findings

### Detection

**AUD-9: END3's IdleStopBackstop inherited the no-clock feed defect (new, post-END-band).** Confidence 90. The replacement backstop is fed from exactly one site: `for await verdict in micGate.verdicts`, a deduped change-only stream; `onMicLevel` is explicitly "currently unconsumed" (`MeetingRecorder.swift:36-44`, `MeetingSessionController.swift:60-68`). After silence causes one flip to `.silentByRMS`, no further verdicts arrive, `ingest` never runs again, and the 8-min nudge and 15-min auto-stop never fire on the exact scenario they exist for (forgotten recording in a silent room). Same structural defect the deleted MicOnlySilenceBackstop had (its 2 lifetime firings were both churn artifacts). Fix: also feed `ingest` from the existing ~1 Hz `onMicLevel`/`onSystemLevel` callbacks or a 1 Hz tick.

**AUD-10: END2 residuals: correlated co-fire still confirms instantly; device-idle corroborator not landed.** Confidence 75 (runtime frequency unproven). The 2026-06-09 repro's fragments 1 and 4 were two-signal CONFIRMED (`confirmed_by: ["shareable_content_window_gone"]`): ax-leave and window-gone fail together on a Teams share re-render, and the fast path (`handleEndingProvisional`, any second `.ended` promotes immediately, no debounce, no corroboration check) still has that shape at HEAD. END2-as-landed guards only the lone-ax-leave tick path, plus the AX re-walk (which may prevent ax-leave from leading on transient staleness; untested live). The genuinely independent corroborator END2's own spec called for (`kAudioDevicePropertyDeviceIsRunningSomewhere`) did not ship. Fix: treat ax-leave + window-gone as one evidence class for confirmation, apply `requiresCorroboration` to the fast path, land device-idle.

**AUD-11: ShareableContentSignal probes block the MAIN thread behind an untimed semaphore at 1-2 Hz all meeting.** Confidence 88. `defaultProbe` waits on an untimed `DispatchSemaphore` around `SCShareableContent.getExcluding...`; `defaultScheduler` is a main-runloop Timer; the in-code "scheduler dispatches probes off main" comment is false. Repeated blocking IPC on the thread that owns the HUD, prompt, hotkeys, and every detection timer, precisely during screen share; a hung SCK call (already documented in-repo for `stopCapture`, `MeetingRecorder.swift:833-836`) freezes the daemon with recording running and no possible stop. `rescueProvisionalEnd`'s synchronous AX DFS has the same shape. Fix: dedicated serial queue (mirror `MeetingDiscoveryWatcher.scanQueue`) or bounded `semaphore.wait(timeout:)` returning nil.

**AUD-12: Prompt panel invisible over full-screen meeting Spaces + timeout default skip = attended meeting, zero recording, zero feedback.** Confidence 72 (AppKit behavior is standard but not runtime-verified here; exposure depends on full-screen habits). The panel lacks `.fullScreenAuxiliary` (so does the HUD); the 30s timeout applies default "skip" silently; with AUD-1/2 the suppression can then outlive the meeting. One-line collectionBehavior fix + a timeout-skip notification (see AUD-16). Evidence: `MeetingPromptWindow.swift:106-107`, `MeetingSessionController.swift:352-371`, `ConfigStore.swift:102`.

### Recording and processing

**AUD-13: Mid-meeting SCStream death is silent and unrecoverable.** Confidence 85. `didStopWithError` only logs: no degraded banner (wired to start-failure only), no event, `retrySystemAudio` no-ops (`systemCapture` stays non-nil), no system-side liveness watchdog (mic has one), and the stop-time "mic-only" warning is gated on `lastSystemFires == 0` so a stream that died at minute 5 of 60 produces nothing. Everything others said after the death point is silently absent. Triggers: TCC revocation (routine here per the cdhash churn), display reconfigure, SCK daemon failure. Evidence: `SystemAudioCapture.swift:209-212`, `MeetingRecorder.swift:104-105, 391-414, 683-693`, `MeetingSessionController.swift:293-295`. Fix: propagate `didStopWithError` to `onSystemAudioDegraded` (banner + retry become live mid-meeting for free), add a `systemFires` watchdog, gate the stop-time warning on duration coverage, not `== 0`.

**AUD-14: Total publish failure exits 0; daemon clears the failure sidecar and can notify "Meeting published" with a stale page URL.** Confidence 82. `fanout` never raises; `orchestrate` exits 0 with `publish_state: "none"`; `SinkDispatcher` emits `pipeline_succeeded` + clears `error.json`; the notification reads the PREVIOUS run's `notion.json` URL on a failed re-publish. The Library badge is honest; the primary UX channel lies. Retry also re-summarizes (pays twice) instead of re-publishing. Evidence: `publish_router.py:146-149`, `orchestrate.py:401-403, 510-515`, `SinkDispatcher.swift:135-143`, `PipelineLauncher.swift:191-193`. Fix: distinct exit code (or result payload) when `publish_state == "none"`; stage-aware retry.

**AUD-15: Library Republish and BYO publish-from-paste bypass workflow sink routing entirely.** Confidence 92. Both call `publish_notion.publish()` directly: a workflow deliberately configured `sinks=["obsidian"]` (private but not NDA) gets a real Notion page on one click, and the configured sinks never receive the summary. NDA/regulated stay protected by the short-circuit + egress guard; everything else routes wrong. The daemon-side docstring claims fanout. Evidence: `publish_from_paste.py:23,232`, `publish_notion.py:133-138`, `PipelineLauncher.swift:205`, `MeetingLibraryService.swift:74,87`. Fix: route both through `publish_router.fanout` (`mp publish` already exists and honors the overlay).

**AUD-16: The silent-failure family: skip-runs masquerade as success; stranded jobs; timeout-skip invisible.** Confidence 90/90/82. (a) A `no_speech` run exits 0 writing nothing; the daemon posts "Meeting processed - Local Markdown ready (regulated mode)" (wrong twice), the row shows Processing for 2h, then decays to a generic Failed whose Retry recreates the dead state forever (`orchestrate.py:248-261`, `Notifier.swift:115-118`, `MeetingStore.swift:390-397`). (b) Daemon restart strands queued/in-flight jobs: in-memory queue, no startup reconciliation sweep, stale Processing rows excluded from the failed badge, no notification; recovery affordance exists (inline Retry) but the user is never told it is needed (`SinkDispatcher.swift:26-27`, `PipelineFailureSidecar.swift:4`). (c) Timeout-skip discards a detected meeting with no surface at the loss moment, while every comparable failure branch notifies (`MeetingSessionController.swift:365-372`). Fix: per-outcome terminal states (`.skipped`/`.empty`), a startup sweep that re-enqueues or writes `error.json("interrupted")`, a timeout-skip notification with a start-late action.

**AUD-17: SCShareableContent cached at launch is never invalidated; start and every retry can fail against a stale display.** Confidence 65 (whether SCK fails on a stale SCDisplay vs resolving transparently is device/OS-dependent and needs a repro). Cache is static, set once, never cleared; no display-reconfiguration observer exists; retry reuses it. Evidence: `SystemAudioCapture.swift:27-31, 130-146`. Fix: clear + re-fetch on start failure or display reconfigure (re-fetch does not re-prompt TCC once granted).

### Mute gate / capture-first residuals

**AUD-18: The runaway-withhold guard measures whole-file energy with an 85% cliff: it kills legitimate redaction on heavy-mute meetings AND lets sub-85% stale timelines destroy speech.** Confidence 84. `micChannelMeanDb` averages the whole channel (no span windowing): 5 min of speech in 60 min yields about -36 dBFS > -50 floor, so any opt-in meeting muted >= 85% (the canonical all-hands use case) always withholds redaction, silently (log-only). Inversely, a stale timeline covering 84% destroys 50 minutes of real speech with the guard never engaging. The discriminator is measuring the wrong thing. Evidence: `MuteRedactor.swift:24,30,206-207,228-241`. Below 85: latent until a workflow opts in (none currently do). Fix: per-span energy (windowed by the muted spans themselves); withhold or trim suspect spans, not the whole redaction; surface `mute_redaction_withheld` via notification. Relates to TECH-MIC9 (extends, does not duplicate).

**AUD-19: originals/ has no retention policy or reaper (ADR 0016 explicitly requires both); meeting deletion leaves the full original behind; quarantine path skips backup exclusion.** Confidence 92 (two domains found it independently). Every redaction and every quarantined orphan deposits a full unredacted WAV into `originals/` forever; `softDeleteMeeting` trashes only `raw/` stem files; nothing ever deletes from originals; the orphan-quarantine write path sets 0600 but not `isExcludedFromBackup` (so quarantined full audio reaches Time Machine, contradicting the MIC5 DONE claim). The most sensitive artifact the app produces is unbounded, invisible in every UI, and survives user deletion. Evidence: ADR `0016:75`, `MuteRedactor.swift:36-46,174-190`, `OrphanRecordingRecovery.swift:119-125`, `MeetingLibraryService.swift:228-263`. Fix: age/size-bounded reaper + cascade delete with the meeting + two-line backup-exclusion fix in quarantine.

### Local models / transcription

**AUD-20: Interrupted MLX model download is permanently reported as completed.** Confidence 88. `isCached` returns true for any non-empty snapshot dir; `ensure()` then short-circuits without spawning, so the Python byte-count check that would catch partial caches is structurally unreachable (each side's comment declares the other authoritative). First local summarize then has to fetch multi-GB remainders inside 120s timeouts, failing opaquely while the menu bar says the model is ready. Competitor changelogs (Superwhisper) show this exact bug class shipping repeatedly. Evidence: `ModelDownloadSupervisor.swift:32,42-45,55-59`, `LocalModelPreloader.swift:38`. Fix: always spawn prefetch (its real check is cheap when cached) or verify completeness (no `*.incomplete` blobs + full snapshot manifest).

**AUD-21: Local backend hardcodes 120s HTTP/startup timeouts that contradict the system's own documented latencies.** Confidence 75 (latency is hardware-dependent; the contradiction is in-repo). The Swift watchdog budgets 20 min for a 32B run; the Python client caps every completion at 120s with no config knob; timeout fires before the schema-retry layer engages, and the auto backend's local fallback leg inherits it. Evidence: `summarize_local.py:135`, `PipelineLauncher.swift:221`, `summarize.py:309-314`. Fix: plumb both timeouts through config; scale read timeout with max_tokens.

**AUD-22: Apple Intelligence backend lifts the long-meeting guard on a chunking claim its single uncapped reduce cannot honor.** Confidence 82 (needs a macOS 26 repro for the exact context ceiling). Map windows are capped at 3200 chars for a ~4K-token model, but the reduce concatenates ALL partials into one prompt; the only backend exempted from `skip_above_chars` is the one with the smallest context and a fail-fast window loop. Evidence: `orchestrate.py:288-291`, `AppleIntelligenceSummarizer.swift:23-24,39,119-125`. Fix: hierarchical reduce (re-chunk partials until one window), tolerate per-window failures.

### Security

**AUD-23: `mp dogfood` bypasses the entire regulated/NDA zero-egress regime.** Confidence 88. It loads config + secrets and unconditionally POSTs the raw transcript to Anthropic: no `apply_workflow_overrides`, no `arm_for_config`, so neither the policy nor the structural httpx block engages. Every other LLM entry point arms the guard. Evidence: `dogfood.py:92,311-320`, `egress_guard.py:102`. Fix: arm the guard + apply the overlay at dogfood entry; skip the cloud leg under regulated/NDA.

**AUD-24: Cross-language sidecar contract has no shared test and the reader is fail-open.** Confidence 75 (keys verified matching TODAY; the finding is that only convention keeps them matching). Swift and Python each test their own literals; `apply_overrides` falls back to globals on any miss, so a renamed key silently turns a local-only workflow into publish-to-Notion-via-Anthropic. The chunker got a golden fixture (ARCH4); the higher-stakes contract did not. Evidence: `MeetingMetaSidecarTests.swift:43-50`, `test_workflow_overlay.py:18,108`, `workflow.py:61-94`. Fix: one fixture directory, two readers (Swift writes three shapes, both suites assert).

## Architecture review

(Method note: this domain's workflow finder died twice, first on an API error, then the resume was killed by overnight machine sleep. It was salvaged as a direct architect-agent run whose findings were then verified inline by the main session against HEAD. The architect independently re-found AUD-1, AUD-6, AUD-9, and AUD-11 without being told their details beyond one-line exclusions, which is decent cross-validation of those four.)

**AUD-26: PromotionEngine phase machine has no escape from terminal `.ended` while the app layer holds a different state.** This is the structural statement of AUD-1: the engine and the app state machine (`AppState`) can disagree permanently because engine resets happen only on idle-transition disengage and stopRecording. A state machine pairing should make illegal state pairs unrepresentable or self-healing; today the pair (`engine .ended`, `app .suppressed`) is stable and dead. The fix in AUD-1 (reset/re-engage on withheld ends) is the minimal wrap; the durable shape is an explicit invariant check: any verdict the app layer drops must put the engine in a state that can re-emit.

**AUD-27: Main-thread concentration of detection I/O.** All detection timing (0.25s engine tick, 1 Hz AX health poll, SC poll at 0.5-1 Hz) runs on the main run loop, and two of the probes do blocking cross-process IPC (AUD-11, the rescue AX DFS). The pattern that already exists in-repo (`MeetingDiscoveryWatcher.scanQueue`) is the right one; detection signal probes should live on a serial utility queue with main-hops only for UI. One stalled WindowServer call currently stalls recording control.

**AUD-28: The five-sidecar file contract has no schema versioning and three undocumented members.** `meta/run/notion/error/summary` plus the newer `mute-timeline.json` and `.capturemode` marker: none carries a `schema_version`, and the latter two plus `originals/` are absent from CONVENTIONS.md/ARCHITECTURE.md's on-disk inventory (the error sidecar IS documented, so the precedent exists). Old-sidecar-meets-new-code is currently handled by fail-open defaults (AUD-24 shows why that is the wrong default for routing fields). Cheap fix: add `schema_version: 1` to writers now, document the three members; readers warn on unknown versions.

**AUD-29: `disengage()` mutates the PromotionEngine off its serialization queue; queued events can corrupt the fresh state after reset.** Severity major, confidence 85 (mechanism code-certain; runtime frequency unproven). Every engine mutation (`ingest`, `tick`, `confirmRecording`) is dispatched through `engineQueue.async`, but `disengage()` calls `engine.reset()` directly on main, and the class doc claims "serialised on engineQueue so the engine's phase stays consistent", which is false at exactly this call site (the same doc-vs-code mismatch pattern MIC2 fixed in MicGate). `adapter.stop()` does not flush events already queued: a burst of `.ended` events from the dying adapter can execute AFTER the reset, re-mutating the fresh `.idle` engine into a phantom `.endingProvisional`/`.ended` that poisons the next engagement; it is also an unsynchronized cross-thread write to a plain `var phase`. Evidence: `MeetingLifecycleCoordinator.swift:10` (doc claim), `:33` (queue), `:87-93` (`disengage` calling `engine.reset()` inline), `:123,132,142` (the three `engineQueue.async` sites). Fix: route the reset through `engineQueue` (and have `disengage` bump a generation token the queued blocks check), one small wrap inside the coordinator.

**AUD-30: The Apple Intelligence completion path hardcodes the Notion sidecar and inherits the exit-0 publish contract.** Severity major (shared with AUD-14), confidence 85. Verified at `PipelineLauncher.swift:273-310`: after on-device summarize, the daemon runs `mp publish` (which DOES apply the workflow overlay and arm the egress guard, `publish_cmd.py:36-38`, so routing and zero-egress hold), then unconditionally reads `<stem>.notion.json` for the page URL. For any non-Notion workflow the URL is nil and the success notification falls into the AUD-16a wrong-text family; and `publish_cmd.main` returns 0 even when every sink failed (failures logged, `publish_cmd.py:48-53`), so a fully-failed Apple Intelligence publish completes as success. Fold into the AUD-14 fix: per-sink page-URL resolution + a non-zero exit when `publish_state == "none"`, applied to `mp publish` as well as `run-all`.

**Verdict on "should anything be done differently":** the architecture is fundamentally sound for the product (single daemon + file contracts + subprocess pipeline match the one-user, local-first constraints; the competitor scan validates capture-first and system-audio breadth; the AsyncStream verdict buses and the pure-logic PromotionEngine/MicGate cores are the right shapes and are what makes these fixes cheap). The recurring-bug rate concentrates in four seams, all wrappable without rewrites: (1) the engine/app-state pairing (AUD-1/26), (2) main-thread blocking probes (AUD-11/27), (3) at-stop-only persistence of identity/policy (AUD-6), (4) concurrency discipline at the engine boundary (AUD-29, the one unguarded mutable field left after MIC2/CONC2). No rewrite is recommended; each seam has a bounded wrap named above. Verified-sound, for the record: the ConfigStore TOML round-trip, the runMP factoring (ARCH3), MicGate's lock scoping post-MIC2, SinkDispatcher's serial job queue, and the egress guard's arm placement.

## Competitor scan: what the market says about exactly these problems

- **End detection is unsolved industry-wide; the leaders fuse calendar + process + silence.** Granola: mic-process-inactive OR calendar-end OR ~15 min silence OR sleep, and still false-stops quiet meetings. Otter tightens its silence threshold from 12 to 5 minutes once past the calendar end. Nobody ships a client-side solution better than the END band's direction; the missing ingredient here is **calendar context as an end-hint and pre-title source** (EventKit, on-device, privacy-safe). ADR 0011 disposed CalendarContextSignal; the evidence justifies re-evaluating it as metadata (hints + nudges), not as a detection trigger.
- **Notify-then-confirm is the converged start UX** (Granola, Notion, MacWhisper, Hyprnote). Nobody silently auto-records; meeting-pipe's prompt panel is the right shape. The differentiating add-ons: Hyprnote's "mic busy but not recording" nudge (converts missed starts into recoverable moments) and Fathom's per-meeting-type auto-record policy matrix (calendar-scheduled vs ad-hoc).
- **Silent capture degradation is the #1 real-world failure of system-audio recorders** (Granola's top troubleshooting category: device mismatch, BT dropouts, VPN blocks). This independently ranks AUD-7/13/17 (and the shipped device-change band) as the highest-value reliability work. screenpipe ships per-subsystem health lights; Superwhisper's waveform doubles as a capture-health indicator and its changelog is a free regression catalog (long recordings, device hot-swap, BT reconnect, sub-30s meetings, long-uptime soak).
- **Capture-first with retention is validated; ephemeral-audio is the anti-pattern.** Granola never stores audio, so any failure is an unrecoverable empty note (its loudest field complaint). Notion keeps audio up to 3 days with retry/resume. This endorses ADR 0016's keep-and-redact decision AND makes AUD-19 (retention/reaper) the missing half of a coherent story.
- **No major player offers on-device ASR.** All three leaders stream audio to cloud STT. FluidAudio-on-ANE plus local MLX is a real differentiator; the competitive move is to document the data path per feature (Krisp-style "what goes to the cloud" table) and keep AUD-23-class bypasses closed.
- **Model download is a first-class failure surface everywhere** (Superwhisper shipped four download fixes; MacWhisper documents degenerate-transcript support articles; WhisperKit issues show manifest corruption). AUD-20/21 plus RAM/disk preflight in `mp doctor` are table stakes for the local mode, not polish.
